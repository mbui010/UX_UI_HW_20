Our Goals

We will redesign the website’s IA structure, layout and navigation, to allow people to quickly access and easily find the reliable resources they are looking for.

We want to maintain the brand characteristic as reliable, transparent, integrity, and committed but we will upgrade the design by adding a Minimalistic and tech-oriented style.

Problem Statement

We found that it was difficult to find certain documents and information on the website because it was catergorized under topics that users wouldn’t normally go on. Also, on their career page, it was difficult to find where the apply button is and all of the catergories were present on the home page but no information to determine what each section portrays. Users who are pursuing a career in the security industry have trouble with finding cyber security documents on the NSA site. They also have trouble finding careers and applying to them on the official website. Lastly, if users who have a business that they need cyber security help yet they are struggling to find out more on the site.

We conducted usability tests on the website with our users and their task was to explore the website and find certain documents. We determined that the navigation bar was too confusing to use and that the user usually opted for the search bar.

We organized all of the topics and its contents on this site map. From our user interviews we found that some of the contents did not match well under certain topics so we had to reorganize and move around some informations on the website.

We chose this style guide to keep NSA's professional look as well as it is easy to read and use.

Our Takeaways

After working on the NSA Redesign project we learned a lot with processing a great amount of information and organzing all of them to still included everything they do. Here is what we learned:

1. When going through the website first thing, we should have looked into what NSA actually does see what to priortize more on when redesigning the pages.

2. We should have done more testing because we started to get a little lost on what should be on the pages and what was unnecessary.

3. Carding Sorting and Site Maps are EXTREMELY helpful with organizing all of the data.
